# &#x20;                        **Social Media Checklist** 

### &#x20;                                                *— Responsible Use as a College Student*



* ## **The Do's**



#### \# What to Do:



1. Think for a second before posting anything — would you be okay if your HOD or a future interviewer saw it?

   ---
2. Keep your account privacy settings in check. Platforms quietly reset them after updates, so revisit once in a while.

   ---
3. If you're sharing someone's photo, meme, or write-up — credit them. It takes two seconds and actually matters.

   ---
4. In college groups or academic pages, write the way you'd talk in a classroom. It's still a semi-public space.5Before reposting a "breaking news" or shocking claim, spend 30 seconds verifying it.

   ---
8. Log out of shared/lab devices

   ---
9. ###### Keep your tone consistent across all platforms



* ## **The Don'ts**



#### \# What Not to Do :



1. Don't post when you're angry — about a professor, an exam, or college in general. Vent to a friend instead. A public post stays longer than the feeling does.

   ---
2. Don't share internal stuff — exam papers, college notices, or someone's personal details. Even if it feels harmless, it usually isn't.

   ---
3. Don't use your Instagram story or Twitter to settle a fight. It never ends well and everyone else just ends up watching.

   ---
4. Don't post your live location or announce that you're going home for two weeks. It's a safety thing more than anything else.

   ---
5. Don't assume a DM is private forever. Screenshots exist. Treat it the same way you'd treat a public post.

   ---
6. Don't waste hours arguing with strangers in comment sections

   ---
7. Don't use your college email for random signups

   ---
8. Don't tag people in embarrassing photos without asking

   ---
9. ###### Don't overshare personal problems publicly in the heat of the moment
