### \# General Safety :



### => What to Do-





1. Lock your accounts down — go through your Instagram, Snapchat, and WhatsApp privacy settings right now. Not tomorrow. Most people have never touched them since they signed up.

   ---
2. Use different passwords for different apps — yes it's annoying, yes it matters. If one account gets hacked and you've used the same  password everywhere, they all go down. Use an app like Google Password Manager or just write them down somewhere offline.

   ---
3. Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever it's available — Gmail, Instagram, WhatsApp. It takes 30 seconds to set up and       makes it significantly harder for anyone to break in even if they have your password.

   ---
4. Don't connect to random public Wi-Fi — café Wi-Fi, college canteen hotspots, railway station networks. If you have to use them, avoid logging into anything sensitive like banking apps or email. Use mobile data for that.

   ---
5. Screenshot and save evidence if something feels wrong — if someone is harassing you online, threatening you, or asking for money, don't just block and move on. Screenshot everything first. You may need it later.

   ---
6. ##### Be careful what you share in college groups — department WhatsApp groups, college Discord servers, and online forums are not private spaces. Anything you say there can be forwarded without your knowledge.

### \# Financial \& UPI Safety :



### => What to Do-





1. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank. Banks never ask for these. Ever. If someone calls, texts, or DMs you asking for this information, it is a scam. Hang up. Don't reply.

   ---
2. Be suspicious of any "money request" on UPI apps. Receiving money does not require you to enter your PIN — if someone is telling you to enter your PIN to receive a payment, they are trying to take money from you, not send it. This is one of the most common tricks used on students.

   ---
3. ##### Double-check UPI IDs before sending money — one wrong character and the money goes to a stranger. Most apps don't have a reliable recall process. Verify the recipient's name that shows up on screen before confirming.10Don't fall for "cashback" or "offer" QR codes — if someone sends you a QR code and says scanning it will add money to your account, it won't. QR codes on UPI are for making payments, not receiving them.

#### Don't stay quiet about it. Most students don't report because they feel embarrassed or think nothing will happen. Something usually does, if you report early.
